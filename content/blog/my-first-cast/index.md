## The Setup

The Green River below Flaming Gorge Dam is one of those rivers that gets into your head before you ever arrive. Cold, clear water releases year-round from the base of the dam, keeping temperatures in the low 50s even in midsummer. The trout — mostly browns and rainbows — are well-fed and suspicious.

I arrived at the A-Section parking lot at 6:30 in the morning with a 9-foot 5-weight rod I'd borrowed from a friend, a box of flies I mostly couldn't identify, and a confident ignorance that would be thoroughly dismantled by noon.

## Reading the Water

The first lesson the Green teaches you is patience. The river runs clear enough that you can watch a 20-inch brown hold in two feet of water and inspect every fly you present. If your drift is off by six inches, the fish simply slides back under its undercut bank and you're left staring at ripples.

I spent the first hour making what I thought were elegant casts. They were not. The wind caught my leader on nearly every backcast, piling the fly in a heap six feet short of my target. A guide floating by in a drift boat offered the only feedback I'd get all morning: a sympathetic nod.

## The Hatch

Around 10 a.m. the Pale Morning Duns came off. If you've never watched a trout hatch develop, it's a slow-motion explosion — first one rise ring, then five, then thirty fish feeding across a hundred-yard stretch of river. The air fills with small yellowish mayflies and the water surface begins to dimple everywhere at once.

I tied on a PMD pattern — a size 18 parachute that looked approximately right — and made my best cast toward a steady riser holding just off a weed bed. The fly landed clean, rode the current for about two feet, and the fish took it.

I lifted the rod. The fish was gone. I had struck too late, or too hard, or both.

## What I Took Home

I didn't land a fish that day. I did land a better understanding of the things I didn't know: slack-line casts, leader turnover, the difference between a refusal and a bad drift. I also took home a deep respect for anyone who makes this look effortless.

The Green will be there next weekend. I'll be back with a size 20 Sparkle Dun and more humility than I brought this time.
